Research Contribution

Adaptive Actuator Intelligence

Four novel contributions closing the sim-to-real gap at the actuator level — not the policy level.

C1 — CONTRIBUTION

Self-Profiling Motor Characterization

Auto-identification of R, L, λ, cogging torque map, and encoder offset on first boot. No datasheets required — the controller learns its own motor.

C2 — CONTRIBUTION

Fused Learned Compensator

Fixed-point neural network running inside the 20 kHz FOC ISR. <5μs inference on CORDIC hardware. Compensates cogging, friction, and thermal drift in real-time.

C3 — CONTRIBUTION

Digital Twin Telemetry

CAN-FD streaming of calibrated actuator state at 5 Mbps. Full torque, velocity, position, temperature — everything the simulator needs to close the loop.

C4 — CONTRIBUTION

Sim-to-Real at the Actuator Level

Inspired by arXiv:2410.16591 — GRU-based torque estimation achieving 32% cost-of-transport reduction. The actuator model, not the policy, is the bottleneck.

Hardware Platform

TITAN Bipedal Robot

A 4-DOF quasi-direct-drive biped designed for locomotion research. Every joint is a TITAN_FOC controller driving a T-Motor through a cycloidal gearbox.

  • 4-DOF — hip + knee pitch per leg
  • T-Motor U5 Kv60 actuators
  • 8:1 cycloidal reduction (30mm, 3D printed)
  • 0.25m + 0.25m link lengths
  • Passive ankle springs (Phase 1)
  • CAN-FD daisy-chain to host PC
~$1,700 total BOM cost
